# Formalizer — Tone Examples

One short before/after per tone (21 entries). Use these to anchor your rewrite when consistency is shaky. Source text is the same across all examples for easier comparison.

**Source (used for all examples below):**

> The thing is, the report's late and I'm pretty annoyed because I asked for it last Tuesday and now I have to redo my whole presentation. Can you send it today?

---

## Formal registers

### professional

> The report is overdue. I requested it last Tuesday, and the delay has required me to revise my presentation. Could you send it today?

*Removes filler ("the thing is"), neutralises emotion, retains directness.*

### formal

> The report remains outstanding. As it was requested on Tuesday of last week, its non-receipt has necessitated a complete revision of the accompanying presentation. I would appreciate its delivery today.

*No contractions, elevated vocabulary, complex clauses.*

### technical

> The report is past its requested delivery date (last Tuesday). Its absence is blocking dependent work — specifically, the presentation, which now requires rework. Please deliver today.

*Precise, structured, dependency-aware.*

### academic

> The report appears to be overdue: it was requested on the previous Tuesday and has not yet been received. This delay has necessitated revisions to subsequent dependent work. A timely delivery would be appreciated.

*Hedged ("appears to be"), passive tolerated, third-person leaning.*

### legal

> The Report, requested on Tuesday last, remains undelivered. Said non-delivery has required the Recipient to undertake substantial revisions to dependent materials. The Sender is hereby requested to deliver the Report on the date hereof.

*Defined terms capitalised, archaic constructions ("said", "hereof"), formal address.*

## Informal and emotional

### informal

> The report's late and it's thrown my whole presentation off — I asked for it last Tuesday. Any chance you can send it over today?

*Contractions, casual rhythm, friendly close.*

### polite

> Apologies for following up — I just wanted to check in on the report I asked for last Tuesday. The delay has meant some extra work on my end with the presentation, so if you could send it today I'd really appreciate it.

*Soft openings, gratitude, respectful framing.*

### less snarky

> The report is late. I asked for it last Tuesday and the delay has meant I need to redo my presentation. Please send it today.

*Strips frustration, keeps the facts and the request.*

### angry

> The report is over a week late. I asked you for it last Tuesday — now I'm redoing the entire presentation because of this. Send it today.

*Punchy, urgent, direct accusation, no softening.*

### calm

> The report is overdue from last Tuesday. The delay has required revisions to the presentation. Please send it today.

*All emotional charge removed; matter-of-fact.*

### passionate

> I really need this report today — it's been a week, and the presentation depends on it. I've put real work into making this land, and I need your piece to bring it together. Please get it to me.

*Conviction, investment, direct appeal.*

### sarcastic

> Just checking — that report I asked for last Tuesday, the one I've now redone half my presentation without, any chance it's coming today? Or should I pencil in next month?

*Understatement, irony, edged.*

### sociable

> Hey! Quick check-in on that report I asked about last Tuesday — I've ended up reworking the presentation a bit while waiting, so any chance you could send it through today? Would be a huge help, thanks!

*Warm, conversational padding, friendly close.*

### empathetic

> I know things have been busy on your end. The report I asked for last Tuesday hasn't come through yet, and I've had to redo a fair bit of the presentation while waiting. If you could send it today, it would really help me get back on track.

*Acknowledges the other side first, then states impact and request.*

### diplomatic

> I wanted to follow up on the report from last Tuesday, in case it slipped through. The presentation has had to be reworked in the meantime, so receiving it today would be ideal if at all possible.

*Face-saving ("in case it slipped"), hedged request, no direct accusation.*

## Structural

### accessible

> The report is late. I asked for it last Tuesday. I had to redo my presentation. Please send it today.

*Short sentences, common words, no jargon.*

### readable

> The report is late — I asked for it last Tuesday, and now I've had to redo my whole presentation. Could you send it today?

*Same vocabulary tier, just smoother flow.*

### concise

> The report is a week late. Send it today.

*Cut everything inessential.*

### grammatical

> The thing is, the report is late, and I'm pretty annoyed because I asked for it last Tuesday and now I have to redo my whole presentation. Can you send it today?

*Fixes "the report's" → "the report is" elision but keeps everything else.*

### bullets

- Report is overdue (requested last Tuesday).
- Delay has required redoing the presentation.
- Please send today.

*One idea per bullet, dash-prefixed, scannable.*

### thesaurus

*(Thesaurus operates on a target word, not a sentence. Example with target word "annoyed":)*

> frustrated

*Single best synonym, no commentary.*

## Domain-specific

### marketing

> Your report unlocks my presentation. I asked for it last Tuesday — and every day without it costs me rework. Send it today and we both ship on time.

*Benefit-led, second-person, punchy, clear next action.*

### irish-english

> The report's late and I'm fairly annoyed — I'd asked for it last Tuesday and now I've had to redo the whole presentation. Any chance you could send it today?

*"Fairly" instead of "pretty", "any chance you could" idiom, no US-specific phrasing. Maintains the source register.*
